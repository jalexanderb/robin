Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026

RobinHealth is in beta. This policy describes how we handle your information today. For our specific commitments around health information, please also read our Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy.

RobinHealth ("RobinHealth," "we," "us") helps people understand and push back on confusing or excessive medical bills. You share your bill with us, and we analyze it, explain it, and — with your authorization — help you dispute, appeal, or negotiate it. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices and rights you have. We only collect what we need to do this work for you.

1. Information we collect

We collect information you provide and information generated as we help you:

  • Documents you upload — your medical bill and, if you choose, your insurer's Explanation of Benefits (EOB).
  • Information from those documents — provider/facility name and address, account number, dates of service, charge line items and billing codes, and amounts.
  • Information you tell us — your name (for letters), approximate household income and size (to check financial-assistance eligibility), your insurance status, and answers to questions that help us build your strategy.
  • Contact details you provide — e.g. a provider's or insurer's email or fax, so we can send correspondence on your behalf when you ask us to.
  • Case activity — the analysis, letters, negotiation status, and outcomes.
  • Technical data — a per-case access token, basic request logs (IP, timestamps), and a short-lived browser entry that stores only your case identifier so you can resume. We do not store your bill or health details in your browser.

We do not use third-party advertising trackers or analytics pixels.

2. How we use your information

  • To read and analyze your bill, find likely errors and overcharges, and estimate savings.
  • To recommend a strategy, draft letters and appeals, and — with your authorization — contact your provider and/or insurer on your behalf.
  • To answer your questions about your bill, insurance, and your rights.
  • To operate, secure, and improve the service, and to meet legal obligations.

3. AI processing

We use a third-party AI provider, Anthropic (the maker of Claude), to read and analyze the documents and questions you submit. Anthropic processes this data on our behalf as a service provider and, under its commercial terms, does not use it to train its models. We send only what's needed to perform the analysis you've asked for.

4. Who we share it with

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only with:

  • Service providers (subprocessors) that run the service for us — currently Anthropic (AI processing), Railway (application hosting and database), and Vercel (frontend hosting) — under contracts that limit them to providing those services.
  • Your provider and/or insurer — only when you authorize us to send a letter, appeal, or negotiation on your behalf, and only the information needed for that purpose.
  • Legal and safety — if required by law, or to protect rights, safety, or the integrity of the service.

5. How we protect your information

  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and encryption at rest for our database.
  • Each case is protected by a unique access token, so only you (or someone you share it with) can view or act on your case.
  • Access controls, rate limiting, and an automatic session timeout.

No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we work to protect your information and to limit what we keep.

6. How long we keep it

We keep your case information while your case is active and for a limited period afterward, then delete it on a scheduled basis. The most sensitive raw text of your bill is not retained after we've extracted the structured details we need. You can ask us to delete your data at any time (see below).

7. Your choices and rights

  • Delete your data — you can delete your case and all associated data at any time from within the app, or by emailing us.
  • Access and correction — you can ask what we hold about you and ask us to correct it.
  • Withdraw consent — you can stop using the service and withdraw your consent to processing at any time.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) — including the right to know, delete, and not be discriminated against for exercising your rights. We do not sell your personal information or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising. Washington, Nevada, and Connecticut residents: please see our Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy.

8. Children

RobinHealth is not directed to children and we do not knowingly collect information from children under 16.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the service evolves. We'll revise the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, provide a more prominent notice.
