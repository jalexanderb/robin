Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026

This policy is specifically about consumer health data and is provided in addition to our general Privacy Policy. It addresses rights under the Washington My Health My Data Act (MHMDA), Nevada SB 370, and the Connecticut Data Privacy Act's health-data provisions. If anything here conflicts with the general Privacy Policy for consumer health data, this policy controls.

Because RobinHealth works with your medical bills and insurance documents, some of the information we handle is "consumer health data" — information linked to you that identifies your past, present, or future physical or mental health status (for example, the procedures and diagnoses implied by the billing codes on your bill). This policy explains how we treat it.

1. Consumer health data we collect

  • Your medical bill and (optionally) your Explanation of Benefits, including the service line items, billing codes (CPT/HCPCS/revenue codes), dates of service, and the provider or facility involved — which can indicate the care you received.
  • Information you share about your situation (for example, whether care was an emergency, your insurance status, and your household income and size for financial-assistance checks).

2. How we collect it

Directly from you — the documents you upload and the answers you give us in the app. We do not buy consumer health data, and we do not use technologies that geofence health-care facilities.

3. Why we use it

  • To analyze your bill, identify likely errors and overcharges, and estimate potential savings.
  • To build your strategy and, with your authorization, draft and send disputes, appeals, and negotiations to your provider or insurer.
  • To answer your questions and to operate and secure the service.

We use it only for these purposes — the ones you came to us for — not for advertising.

4. Who we share it with

We do not sell your consumer health data. We share it only with:

  • Processors who run the service for us under contract: Anthropic (AI analysis of your documents), Railway (hosting and database), and Vercel (frontend hosting).
  • Your provider and/or insurer, only when you authorize specific correspondence on your behalf, and only the data needed for it.
  • As required by law.

5. Consent

We collect and process your consumer health data only with your consent, which we ask for before you proceed — including consent to have our AI processor analyze your documents. You can withdraw your consent at any time by stopping use of the service and deleting your data; withdrawal does not affect processing already carried out.

6. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Confirm and access the consumer health data we have about you.
  • Delete your consumer health data — available any time from within the app, or by emailing us. We will also direct our processors to delete it.
  • Withdraw consent to our collection and sharing of your consumer health data.

To exercise a right, use the in-app delete option or email privacy@robinhealth.com. We will respond within the time required by law. If we deny a request, you may appeal by replying to our decision; if you are a Washington resident and remain dissatisfied, you may contact the Washington State Attorney General.

7. Data security and retention

We protect consumer health data with encryption in transit and at rest, per-case access tokens, and access controls, and we retain it only as long as needed to provide the service (and a limited period afterward) before deleting it. See our Privacy Policy for details.

8. Contact

Email privacy@robinhealth.com with any question about this policy or your consumer health data.
